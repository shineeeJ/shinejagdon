Longing for Dawn

how much torment must I bear to find the dawn I’ve long pursued? each step echoes through the hollow air, each dream feels faint, subdued. the stars once whispered promises, but now their light feels far, I walk beneath their broken songs, unsure of who I am. my shadow clings, a loyal ghost, it knows the weight I hide, the ache of hope that flickers faint, then drowns beneath the tide.
yet still I move — though tired, though worn — through nights that never cease, seeking just a sliver’s glow, a fractured piece of peace. shall I find it, soft and small, behind the veil of pain? or will I fade before the dawn, and never rise again?
